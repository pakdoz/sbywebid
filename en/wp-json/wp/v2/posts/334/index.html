The Red Bridge was built in the 1890s. The bridge is a link between Jalan Rajawali and Jalan Kembang Jepun. The Red Bridge was built over the Kalimas river. There are at least two versions of the name of the bridge that was built during the Dutch colonial period.

The first version relates to the myth of the shark (Sura) and crocodile (Baya) fighting in Kalimas which caused both of them to bleed and flow through the river. Another version, the name Jembatan Merah was taken to commemorate the Surabaya fighters. Mentioned, in the area there was a fierce battle arek-arek Surabaya against the Allied troops. Around the bridge, Brigadier General Mallaby, leader of the British army; died when a grenade was thrown at his car.
